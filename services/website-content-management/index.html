Common questions

The questions everyone asks first.

Should I outsource my website content management?

If your website is a real part of how customers find you, and you don't have the in-house time to update it monthly — yes. The two failure modes we see are (a) doing it yourself and slowly stopping, or (b) hiring a junior who leaves in 14 months and takes the knowledge with them. Outsourced content management trades a small monthly fee for a site that stays current without your involvement.

What's the difference between content management and content marketing?

Content management is the operational work of keeping your existing pages current — updates, edits, rewrites, image swaps, technical maintenance. Content marketing is the strategic work of creating new content to attract new audiences — blog posts, lead magnets, campaigns. Most small businesses need both, but content management is the foundation. A great marketing campaign on a neglected site converts badly.

How does remote content management actually work?

After a 5-day onboarding (we audit the site, document your tone of voice, set up the publishing workflow), you have a single senior point of contact. Most requests come by email or Slack. You don't open the CMS. We send a monthly one-page report covering what changed, what it moved, and what we're recommending next. No dashboards, no client portal, no software to learn.

How much does outsourced content management cost?

Our Content Care Plan starts at $499/month and covers the core content management work — page updates, monthly blog post, image management, technical maintenance, monthly reporting. SEO and Google Business Profile management add to that depending on scope. Most founder-led clients land between $499 and $1,800/month. We're upfront about pricing before any audit; no quote-on-call surprises.

Am I locked in?

No. Every plan is month-to-month. You can pause or cancel any month with no notice. 93% of our clients renew anyway, which we think is the more honest measure of whether the work is worth it.
